The Profit Pulse concept is a **business idea evaluation framework** designed for solo founders and micro-entrepreneurs. Its core purpose is to provide a systematic, rapid, and objective way to assess whether an idea is worth pursuing, thereby cutting through "shiny object syndrome" and "analysis paralysis."

Its primary utility is to generate **executable clarity**, allowing an operator to move from an idea to a validated, income-generating asset quickly and with minimal resource waste. It is built for small bets, fast feedback, and real revenue signals, prioritizing lean, low-/no-code execution.Profit Pulse App Functionality and Components

The web application is specified as a static, single-page tool designed to be deployed and sold commercially. Its core functions are:

1. **Idea Input Form:** Collects essential data, including the idea name, one-sentence description, the three quantitative scores (Quickness, Profitability, Validation Ease), and the qualitative **Energy Filter** response.  
2. **Calculation Engine:** Implements the weighted QPV formula to generate a quantitative score.  
3. **Report Generation:** Automatically creates a structured, comprehensive evaluation report.  
4. **Idea Management Dashboard:** Allows users to save, load, compare, and manage multiple evaluated ideas using local storage (no backend for v1).  
5. **Export Functionality:** Enables users to save their complete evaluations as **Markdown** (for tools like Notion or Obsidian) and **JSON** (for re-importing data). PDF export is planned as an enhancement.

\-----Detailed QPV Analysis and Scoring Criteria

The Profit Pulse framework relies on two mandatory components used in sequence: the QPV Matrix (Quantitative Score) and the Energy Filter (Qualitative Gate).1. QPV Matrix (Quantitative Score)

This matrix scores the idea across three weighted dimensions (0‚Äì10 scale) to produce a single, weighted **QPV Score**.

| Dimension | Weight | What It Measures | Consideration for Scoring |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Quickness (Q)** | **40%** | How fast a testable version can be shipped. | 0 \= Months of work | 10 \= Live in 48 hours |
| **Profitability (P)** | **30%** | The revenue potential relative to the effort required to maintain and scale it. | 0 \= Unlikely to monetize | 10 \= Clear $1k+/month path |
| **Validation Ease (V)** | **30%** | How quickly real demand signals (pre-sales, signups) can be acquired and tested. | 0 \= Hard to validate | 10 \= Pre-sales possible in 24 hours |

**Weighted QPV Formula:**  
$$ \\text{Weighted QPV Score} \= (\\text{Q} \\times 0.4) \+ (\\text{P} \\times 0.3) \+ (\\text{V} \\times 0.3) $$

**Score Interpretation:** The final score is translated into an immediate call to action:

* **8.0‚Äì10.0:** "Exceptional ‚Äî launch this now"  
* **6.0‚Äì7.9:** "Strong ‚Äî prioritize within 1-2 weeks"  
* **4.0‚Äì5.9:** "Moderate ‚Äî validate further before committing"  
* **0.0‚Äì3.9:** "Weak ‚Äî reconsider or pivot significantly"

2\. Energy Filter (Qualitative Gate)

The qualitative gate is applied *after* the quantitative score. It prevents the operator from building a profitable product they will ultimately resent or abandon, addressing post-launch burnout.

**The Filter Question:"If this made $500/month, would you still be proud to maintain it?"**

**Classification Logic:** The user's response is mapped to one of three statuses:

* **Pass:** The idea is aligned with values and sustainable long-term (`Yes` response).  
* **Fail:** The idea is misaligned and not worth maintaining even if profitable (`No` response).  
* **Revise:** The scope, audience, or business model needs adjustment before a final decision can be made (`Maybe` response).

The Complete Evaluation Report

The final utility of the app is the comprehensive, auto-generated report that translates the scores into concrete next steps. This report always includes:

1. **QPV Score Breakdown**  
2. **Energy Filter Result** (Status and rationale)  
3. **Validation Plan** (3‚Äì7 specific, 24‚Äì72 hour checks)  
4. **Suggested Launch Stack** (Specific low-code/no-code tool recommendations, e.g., Carrd ‚Üí Tally ‚Üí Airtable ‚Üí Zapier ‚Üí Gumroad)  
5. **48-Hour Execution Plan** (A step-by-step, timeboxed checklist from start to a live, testable version).
